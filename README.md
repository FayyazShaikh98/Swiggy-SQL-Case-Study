# Swiggy SQL Case Study

This repository contains a PDF version of a PowerPoint presentation analyzing Swiggy’s business operations using SQL. The case study demonstrates how SQL can be applied to extract meaningful insights from data and support data-driven decisions. The analysis focuses on key business metrics including restaurant revenue, top customers, delivery partner ratings, and popular food items.

# Contents

Swiggy_SQL_Case_Study.pdf – Complete presentation with detailed analysis, insights, and visualizations.

SQL-based exploration covering:

Restaurant revenue trends to identify high-performing locations

Top customers ranked by order frequency and spending

Average delivery partner ratings to assess service quality

Most popular and frequently ordered food items

# Objectives

Analyze revenue trends to identify growth opportunities.

Recognize top-performing customers and their purchase patterns.

Evaluate delivery partner performance through ratings.

Identify trending and popular food items for menu optimization.

Provide actionable insights to improve customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

# Outcomes

Comprehensive revenue analysis highlighting high-performing restaurants.

Ranked list of top customers with detailed behavior patterns.

Insights into delivery partner performance through rating analysis.

Identification of most frequently ordered and popular food items.

Recommendations for improving operational efficiency and profitability.

# Tools & Techniques

SQL (queries used for analysis)

Data exploration and aggregation techniques

Basic visualizations embedded in the presentation

# Conclusion

This Swiggy SQL Case Study provides a practical example of using SQL for business analytics. It demonstrates how data-driven insights can help improve revenue, optimize operations, enhance service quality, and make informed decisions in a real-world business environment.
